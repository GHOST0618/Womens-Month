Stories that Lift Us

Below are carefully chosen profiles of women whose lives, leadership and courage have changed communities, countries and the world. Each profile contains a short life story, achievements and a quote to reflect on.

South African Icons

Winnie Madikizela-Mandela

Often called the "Mother of the Nation", Winnie Madikizela-Mandela was a fierce anti-apartheid campaigner whose activism spanned decades. She endured arrests, bannings, and exile while raising public awareness and solidarity for the liberation movement.

Impact: Symbol of resistance and women’s leadership in liberation movements; inspired generations of activists.

Quote: "It's not about being fearless, it's about moving forward despite fear."

Dr. Nkosazana Dlamini-Zuma

A medical doctor by training and a veteran politician, Dr. Dlamini-Zuma has held multiple cabinet portfolios and led continental development work. She has been a prominent voice in health, social policy and African diplomacy.

Impact: Leadership across government and pan-African organisations — a visible example of women holding the levers of policy and diplomacy.

Quote: "Public service is about people — their dignity and their future."

Thuli Madonsela

Former Public Protector, Thuli Madonsela is celebrated for her integrity, legal scholarship and commitment to constitutional values. Her investigations and reports strengthened transparency and the rule of law in South Africa.

Impact: Champion of accountability and human rights; role model for law and public service.

Quote: "Courage is not the absence of fear, but the resolve to act in its presence."

Zozibini Tunzi

Crowned Miss Universe 2019, Zozibini used her platform to celebrate natural beauty and advocate for gender equality. She is an advocate for women's empowerment, education and self-acceptance.

Impact: Redefined beauty standards and encouraged young women to embrace confidence and leadership.

Quote: "Own your power, then use it to lift others."

Miriam Makeba

A legendary singer and activist affectionately known as "Mama Africa", Makeba used her voice to draw global attention to apartheid and African cultural pride. Her music bridged activism and artistry.

Impact: Cultural ambassador who fused art with social justice, inspiring artists and activists worldwide.

Quote: "Music can do what politics sometimes cannot."

Dr. Precious Moloi-Motsepe

A business leader, philanthropist and advocate for women’s economic empowerment. Dr. Moloi-Motsepe invests in initiatives that promote education, entrepreneurship and social upliftment.

Impact: Philanthropic work that builds capacity for women entrepreneurs and strengthens communities.

Quote: "When women have opportunities, entire communities thrive."

Global Icons

Malala Yousafzai

An education activist who survived an attack and went on to become the youngest Nobel Peace Prize laureate. Malala campaigns globally for girls’ right to education and equality.

Impact: Global symbol for girls’ education; her story has motivated policy and philanthropic support worldwide.

Quote: "One child, one teacher, one book, one pen can change the world."

Oprah Winfrey

Media entrepreneur and philanthropist whose life story — from hardship to global influence — has inspired millions. Oprah’s leadership in media and giving has highlighted education, healing and possibility.

Impact: Philanthropy that funds education and leadership initiatives; a voice for empowerment through storytelling.

Quote: "Turn your wounds into wisdom."

Michelle Obama

Former First Lady of the United States, lawyer and education advocate. Michelle has used her platform to champion girls’ education, healthy families and leadership development.

Impact: Programs like Reach Higher and Let Girls Learn have inspired millions to pursue education and leadership.

Quote: "There is no limit to what we, as women, can accomplish."

Wangari Maathai

A Kenyan environmentalist and Nobel Peace Prize laureate who founded the Green Belt Movement. Her work connected environmental stewardship with women’s wellbeing and community resilience.

Impact: Pioneered community-driven reforestation and linked environmental justice with women's empowerment.

Quote: "It's the little things citizens do. That's what will make the difference."

Ruth Bader Ginsburg

Late U.S. Supreme Court Justice who spent her career advancing gender equality and civil liberties through law. RBG's legal work and opinions shaped modern understandings of equal protection.

Impact: Landmark legal progress for gender equality and a cultural icon of courage and intellect.

Quote: "Women belong in all places where decisions are being made."

Charlize Theron

South African-born actress and producer who has used global success to fund programs aimed at protecting women's rights and children. Her humanitarian work focuses on ending gender-based violence and supporting survivors.

Impact: Uses influence and resources to support survivors and elevate prevention programs internationally.

Quote: "Be kind, but don't let people use you."

How to draw inspiration from these stories

  • Learn: read their biographies, speeches and interviews to understand their struggles and strategies.
  • Act: volunteer with a women-centred organisation and help amplify local leaders.
  • Share: tell the stories of women in your community — every small voice adds to the movement.
