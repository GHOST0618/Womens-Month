Immediate Help — Helplines

  • Gender-Based Violence Command Centre
    Toll-free: 0800 428 428 | *120*7867# (cellphone USSD) — National helpline for reporting and getting immediate assistance.
  • SADAG (South African Depression & Anxiety Group)
    Helpline: 0800 456 789 | Web: www.sadag.org
    Offers counselling, mental health support and referrals.
  • Childline South Africa
    Toll-free: 0800 055 555 — For children and families in crisis, including child abuse reporting and counselling.
  • Lifeline South Africa
    Phone: 0861 322 322 — Confidential counselling and emotional support via call and online.
  • POWA (People Opposing Women Abuse)
    Hotline: 011 642 4345 | www.powa.co.za — Support for victims of gender-based violence and legal referrals.

If someone is in immediate danger, always call emergency services (10111 in South Africa) or your local emergency number.

Organisations & Programs

Shelters & Protection

Women's shelters provide temporary safe housing and trauma counselling. Local municipalities and NGOs co-ordinate shelter placements — call the GBV Command Centre or local social services for referrals.

Legal assistance: Many NGOs offer free legal referral services for survivors wanting to open cases or receive protection orders. Contact POWA or your provincial Department of Social Development for local options.

Economic Empowerment

Several organisations and government programmes support women entrepreneurs through training, starter grants and mentorship. Look for:

  • Small business development centres
  • Municipal SMME support units
  • Women’s business collectives and co-operatives

If you want help locating a local programme, include your city/town in the contact form below and we’ll point you to nearby resources.

Health & Wellbeing

Women’s health services range from maternal health to mental health and sexual/reproductive health. Your local clinic provides many services free or at low cost; for specialised counselling contact SADAG or Lifeline.

SRH & Maternal Health

Family planning, antenatal care and STI screening are available at public clinics. If you need emergency care, visit your nearest hospital.

Mental health

Emotional wellbeing matters — call SADAG (0800 456 789) or Lifeline (0861 322 322) for counselling and referrals.

Volunteer & Donate

Volunteers and donors are essential to keep shelters, counselling services and training programmes running. Reach out to local NGOs or community centres to offer time, skills or financial support. Even small donations make a difference.

Tip: choose organisations that publish financial accountability reports, and ask how funds are used.
